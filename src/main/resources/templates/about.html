What is Mudman Blues?

In May 2024 I founded Mudman Blues, a solo musical project which mixes loose storytelling with surreal nonsense... and a touch of sobriety, sometimes.

In the catalog, you might find songs in English, Portuguese, and Russian. Although similar in texture, there is a deliberate attempt to let each language bring its own set of nuances. Feel free to explore them and find out what these nuances might be, as I do not intend to name them here, nor am I sure I could.

What is special about this project? My singing isn't particularly good, my playing can be stiff, and the lyrics aren't terrific either. There is, however, a little world that made its way into my music, quite unexpectedly. Characters and places have their own particularities and depths. Picture a place called Geode Rocks, where a man called Mr. Storm and a painter named Andre share laughs, and up in the sky a weasel flies alongside Fluttengton, a flute player travelling atop a giant enchanted pear. I don't know where these things came from, yet I do my best to portray this world, feeling deeply compelled by its imagery.

There is no sorrow without love, and there is no love without sorrow. I support the idea that melancholy is not the lack of hope, insofar as it is the most important step towards hope, as hope can only come from a genuine place, and there is nothing as sincere as a dim sense of sorrow. I must add, however, that the melancholic aspect of Mudman Blues was not a deliberate choice. Come Home, Little Bird was the first song I ever wrote, and from the first line it brought to me the very essence of the project's nature: "once we had the time for desperation..."

I must beg you not to confuse the voice in the songs with the one behind it. Although it’s definitely akin, I have my own flaws, and calling me the Mudman would stray from the project’s original intent. Mudman Blues is, above all, a sonic world shaped by ideas and insecurities that may, in some way, belong to anyone.
